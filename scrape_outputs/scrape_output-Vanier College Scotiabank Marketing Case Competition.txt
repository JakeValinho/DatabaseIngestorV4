The Vanier College Business Administration Department has been hosting and organizing this inter-collegial, national marketing case competition since 2006. The competition offers community college and CEGEP level Business Administration students a singular opportunity to apply what they learn in the classroom to a true to life business experience.
The Vanier College Scotiabank Case Competition is a pressure-cooker event that give students a taste of real world business problems. Teams made up of three students have three hours to analyze a business problem and create a viable marketing plan. They then make a twenty-minute Power Point presentation based on their marketing strategy in front of a panel of judges, peers, and invited guests.
The event is hosted at the Montreal Airport Marriott Hotel over 3 days, featuring awards, dinners and networking opportunities in addition to the competition itself.

Vanier College is pleased to announce that after a gruelling 3-day competition at the 20th Scotiabank Vanier College National Marketing Case Competition, the Gold Award went to Seneca College from Ontario. Congratulations to the Seneca Team composed of students Dominic Lorenzoni, Alexia Afriyie, Tyson Trieu and Marija Kolefski with their Coaches Stefan Kanitz, Domenic Gasparro and Yasmine Shateri. “This has been so special. We just wanted to do well to honour all our hard work. Our team really came together and had great chemistry,” said the students.
During the weekend event, twenty-three teams of marketing students from colleges across Canada had three hours to analyze a real marketing case and present viable marketing solutions before a panel of expert judges. Long-time judge Dave Bussière, Associate Professor, Marketing, Odette School of Business, University of Windsor said, “I think that I have judged the Vanier case competition 15 times -- starting in the second year. I come back every year because it is the single competition that has a national and bilingual feel. It gives students from the West, the Atlantic provinces, Québec and Ontario the chance to interact and really compete. The students are awesome and well-coached! The experience is exhilarating!”
At a gala dinner held on March 1 where the finalists were announced, other awards also recognized coaches and students. This year, the Builders Award went to the original creators of the Vanier Case Competition, David Moscovitz who worked on the competition until he retired from Vanier a few years ago, and George Dracopoulos who has devoted 20 years to the event. Their commitment and engagement have made the competition unique and kept the competition going all these years.
As well, the Julia Ferreira Public Speaking Excellence Awards, named in memory of a Vanier graduate and former case competition participant, was awarded to the three best public speakers of the competition, chosen from each division: Mimi Nguyen, from St Clair College, Katelyn Wynott from Cambrian College and Mason Wright from Durham College.
“The Scotiabank Vanier College National Marketing Case Competition is a wonderful showcase of our students’ abilities and commitment. Indeed, it is deeply inspiring and promising to see the quality of the next generation of business leaders spotlighted here, thanks to Scotiabank, the principal sponsor of this event, and its collaboration with Vanier College,” said Benoit Morin, Director General of Vanier College.
“Scotiabank is proud to support this remarkable platform for bright young minds. This competition reflects Scotiabank’s core values of integrity, inclusion and accountability, and we never cease to be impressed by the students’ work ethic, level of preparation, passion and desire to succeed – it is always very inspiring event!’’ said Paul Yannouris, Regional Director, Small Business Development (Quebec and Eastern Ontario) Scotiabank.
George Dracopoulos, the lead organizer of the Competition, was thrilled with this year’s event. “It’s been 20 years and quite the accomplishment. More than 50 colleges and cegeps from every single province and the Yukon, and 1 500 to 2 000 students have participated and benefited from presenting, meeting business leaders and judges as well as students and coaches from across Canada. This experience will remain with them for a lifetime.”

Again, this year, Vanier College will host the Annual Scotiabank Vanier College National Marketing Case Competition taking place on February 28-March 2, 2025. Twenty-five Colleges and Cegeps from across Canada will be in Montreal to compete in both official languages for this National Collegiate Marketing case competition.
“This will be the 20th edition of the marketing case competition,” says George Dracopoulos, Vanier Case Competition Co-Founder and principal organizer. “Over the years we have had the pleasure of hosting over 50 unique Colleges from coast-to-coast-to coast (from British Columbia, to Newfoundland, to the Yukon). This year, 25 Colleges are registered and primed to compete. Year after year this high-quality event offers a unique opportunity for development and networking to our students. They get the chance to analyze real-world companies and to compete with and meet peers from across the nation.”
Case competitions demand imagination, knowledge, clear thinking, and good presentation skills. To be selected to their teams, students must compete internally at their respective Colleges. The successful candidates demonstrate a combination of analytic capabilities, creativity, and mastery of marketing theory and application. Congratulations to students Callum Stone, Cyrill Jay Paragas, Mathew Connors, and Adriano Palacios (photo left to right) who will join coaches Megan Pepe and Jessica Andrews to form the Vanier team for the 2025 event.
During the Case Competition students, in teams of three, are isolated for three hours to analyze a real-world marketing problem they have never seen before, create a Power Point presentation then present a marketing plan before a judging panel of experts from business, industry and higher education.
The colleges and Cegeps signed up for the 2025 competition are: Cégep de Drummondville, Collège Ahuntsic, Collège Édouard‐Montpetit, Collège André-Grasset, Collège Montmorency, Dawson College, John Abbott College, Marianopolis College, Champlain College, and Vanier College from Québec; Algonquin College, Cambrian College, Durham College, George Brown College, Fanshawe College, Collège La Cité, Niagara College, Seneca College, Sheridan College, and St. Clair College of Applied Arts and Technology from Ontario; Medicine Hat College from Saskatchewan; Southern Alberta Institute of Technology (SAIT) from Alberta; Okanagan College from British Columbia; Nova Scotia Community College from Nova Scotia; and College of the North Atlantic from Newfoundland.
The main competition will take place on Saturday, March 1st, 2025, at the Montreal Airport Marriott in Terminal Hotel), 800 Place Leigh-Capreol, Dorval, QC H4Y 0A4, 514-636-6700. Team presentations begin at 10:00 AM and run until 3:15 PM on Saturday. Because of the large number of participants, there will be three theatres of presentations on Saturday. That evening, the top two teams from each of the three theatres will be announced at a gala dinner at 7:00 PM. These top six teams will then go on to compete in the finals Sunday morning, March 2, 2025, following which, an awards luncheon will take place.
Scotiabank is the principal sponsor of the Scotiabank Vanier College National Marketing Case Competition, and we sincerely thank them for their valuable continued support in student development and career growth for these young exceptional individuals.

After a gruelling 2-day competition, Vanier College is pleased to announce that Cegep Ahuntsic won the Gold Award at the 2024 Scotiabank Vanier College National Marketing Case Competition that featured some of the best business students in Canada on March 2-3, 2024. Congratulations to Gabriel Magnan-Massicotte, Louis-Olivier Racette, Coralie Mill, and Maxime Gendron and their coach Jean-Luc Filiatrault for their winning presentation.
Six teams made it to the Sunday finals on March 3rd where they analyzed a new Marketing case and presented a second time. These were: Cegep Ahuntsic who won the Gold Award, Algonquin College with students Alkira Descoteax, Jaime Miller, Nishanth Babu Battula, and their coaches Melanie Haskins, Craig Flynn, Brenden McBride and Alex Frangione who won Silver; George Brown College that won Bronze with students Torry Tough, Jessica Keffer, and Matthew Spencer with coaches Tom Arhontoudis and John Hendrikx. The other three finalists were Fanshawe College, Niagara College, and Medicine Hat College.
Although they were not in the finals, other teams placed well within their division. In Division 1, St. Clair College came in third place and Marianopolis College, in fourth. In Division 2, Vanier College placed third and Sheridan College placed fourth. In Division 3, Okanagan College placed third and College Edouard-Montpetit placed fourth.
During the weekend event, twenty-four teams of marketing students from colleges across Canada had three hours to analyze a real marketing case and present viable marketing solutions before a panel of expert judges. The finalists on Sunday then had to analyze a new marketing case.
At a Gala dinner held on March 2 where finalists were announced, the Julia Ferreira Public Speaking Excellence Awards, named in memory of a Vanier graduate and former case competition participant, was presented by Dave Bussiere from Windsor University’s Odette School of Business. The awards went to the three best public speakers of the competition, chosen from each division: Mikaël Ouellette, from Collège André-Grasset; Ella Stott, from Champlain College; and Patric Perron, from Collège Édouard-Montpetit.
As well, this year’s Builders Award was given to George Brown College coaches Tom Arhontoudis and John Hendrikx for their commitment and engagement to supporting their students throughout the competition and their spirit of openness and support for all the teams over the weekend event.
“Once again, we delivered an unbelievable event that was greatly appreciated by all attendees and featured high caliber participants from across Canada!” said George Dracopoulos, the principal organizer of the Scotiabank Vanier College National Marketing Case Competition. Echoing the same sentiment, Keith Dundas, coach from the Southern Alberta Institute of Technology, stated “This is such a fine event – the friendly, affable vibe reimagines what a competition can feel like. Well Done! Can’t wait to come back next year!” said
