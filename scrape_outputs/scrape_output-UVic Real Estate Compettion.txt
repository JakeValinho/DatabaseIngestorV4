About Event
​
We are proud to announce the return of the
UVic Real Estate Case Competition
! We are bringing students the opportunity to both interactively learn about real estate and compete in teams for a share of significant prize money.
​
This is your chance to learn about
residential real estate, commercial real estate, and development
, all while applying your knowledge in practical exercises.
​
The Case Competition series consists of
a week of education
through the modules we provide on Brightspace,
a week of preparation
for the case, and a
finale
, where your team of
three or four
will present your proposals to a panel of judges.
​
The exact dates are as follows:
​
March 14
– Modules released on Brightspace
​
March 19
– Case released on Brightspace
​
March 27
– Presentation day (a schedule will be given in advance with your team’s time slot)
​
In addition to the prize money, we hope to offer a
certificate of participation
that helps students relay what they've learned to potential employers.
​
Registration in this competition requires a
commitment to learning, creating, and presenting
. If you register for the competition, we expect you to participate
until
the end.
​
We look forward to seeing you in the competition!

Real Estate Case Competition – Register Now!

Take on a real-world business challenge in our upcoming real estate case competition. Work in a team or get matched with one, learn from expert-curated education modules, and apply your skills in a competition judged by industry professionals. No prior real estate knowledge is needed—this is a great opportunity to gain experience and compete for a share of our $850 prize pool. Open to all university students.

Key Dates:
	•	Education modules released – March 14th
	•	Case released – March 19th
	•	Final presentations – March 27th 7-9pm

In the midst of the busiest month of my short professional career with Studenthaus, my first venture, UVic Real Estate, held its last event of the year and announced its third ever President to succeed Emma French. In its third year of operations, UVic Real Estate held its second annual Case Competition and kicked off Launchpad, a mentorship program designed to give students serious about a career in real estate a path to get there. Addressing the lack of real estate education at UVic was one of the main reasons that I started the organization in summer 2022. This year, everyone on the team helped the club take huge strides forward in that area. I’m super excited to see Charlotte Murray step into the role of President and have no doubt she will continue to grow club success. As for me, I will continue to support her and the rest of the 2025/2026 team as the Chair of the Advisory Board, despite making the move home to Toronto in May.
As my term as President of UVic Real Estate comes to a close, I’ve been reflecting on what an incredible journey this past year has been. It was an honor to lead such a passionate, driven, and inspiring team. I’m deeply grateful to Julian Wells for entrusting me with this role and giving me the privilege of building on the foundation he created. This year, I’m proud to have introduced Launchpad, our first-ever mentorship program connecting UVic students with real estate professionals. Watching mentees grow through those relationships—and seeing several land internships with their mentors’ companies—was one of the most rewarding parts of the role. We also hosted several successful events this year, including our second annual Case Competition, which grew to 25 participants and awarded the winning team scholarships to UDI Capital Region’s Real Estate Development (RaD) Program. Contributing to the growth of this initiative and helping provide students with meaningful industry exposure has been one of the most rewarding aspects of my role. To my incredible team—thank you. Your hard work, creativity, and commitment made all of this possible. It was a privilege to lead alongside such dedicated and talented people. I’ve learned so much from each of you, and I’m so proud of what we accomplished together. Finally, congratulations to Charlotte Murray, the incoming President. I’m so excited to see where you take the organization next—I know it’s in incredibly capable hands.
Economics Student at University of Victoria
