Happening Marketing
In short
Established in 1994, Happening Marketing is the leading interuniversity marketing competition in Eastern Canada. The goal is to excel academically, professionally and personally in the presence of 540 participants passionate about marketing. The event includes 10 categories ranging from quizzes to social concerns, sports and different kinds of marketing cases (B2B, experiential, digital, strategic).

About us
Every year, Happening Marketing (HM) brings together nearly 600 students, teachers, and industry professionals for a weekend during which the most significant marketing competition in Eastern Canada takes place. This competition involves students from 11 universities from the provinces of Quebec and Ontario. The Organizing Committee of HEC Montréal is proud to host the next edition, which will take place on March 22, 23, and 24. This competition encompasses various aspects: academic, sports, social, participation, and contribution. The academic portion is divided into seven cases of different marketing specializations and a knowledge quiz. The sports aspect takes the form of a tournament with mixed teams, and the sport played is non-conventional. As for the social aspect, it consists of various activities related to marketing, mostly aimed at incorporating elements of socialization and mutual aid. In the participation and contribution aspects, the goal is to unite members of each delegation behind their colors to support not only their teams but also a cause determined by the Organizing Committee. Four aspects, a unique opportunity to proudly represent your school. A large-scale competition that will conclude the 2023-2024 year of the RÉFAEC competitions, with a commitment to creating an intelligent event where small details will make a significant difference in the experience of all participants.

The Largest Marketing Case Competition in Eastern Canada
This bilingual competition challenges delegates to show their marketing intelligence, ingenuity, and their spirit. Students who compete at HM have the opportunity to do so in one of four categories: academic, sports, social, or participation. Each team is essential in creating Telfer’s impact in the world of marketing and in bringing home success.
This case focuses on mandates that push participants to create innovative strategies for businesses to reach their future customers.
This case focuses on customer engagement as a form of marketing and combines the company’s product/services with ingenuity to produce captivating experiences for customers.
The strategic case consists of mandates focusing on producing long-term visions for sponsors in terms of their industry and position. Participants will be expected to include the 4 P’s of marketing in their case.
International marketing seeks to analyze the constraints that a company may face in the context of globalization. Taking into account global operational differences, similarities, as well as opportunities in order to achieve global objectives.
In this case, participants create their own agency a week before the competition. Competitors will also design a media communications plan for their agency that will respond to the needs of the case.
The quiz category tests participants' knowledge of all marketing-related content. Questions asked during quiz involve knowledge of logos, slogans, and much more according to the subject choices of the teams. This category is best fitted for highly competitive individuals.
This case challenges delegates to use their critical thinking skills in digital marketing to attract engagement with the sponsor’s business.
The surprise case tests candidates’ versatility and knowledge of marketing. The case will be given under a surprise category, meaning all participants in this role must have strong knowledge in a wide range of marketing strategies. All resources are allowed to complete the mandate of this case, and participants are given advanced time to work on the case before the competition to ensure high-caliber results.

Case competitions at Telfer
Case competitions offer a platform for you to apply classroom learning to real-world business challenges. Participants form teams analyze a business scenario, develop a strategy and pitch their ideas to a panel of industry experts. Competitions enhance problem-solving skills, foster teamwork and provide an opportunity to network with industry professionals.
Case competitions in numbers
Benefits of case competitions
Engage in case competitions to immerse yourself in real-world business challenges, mirroring the intensity and dynamics of professional environments. These experiences will sharpen your essential skills, such as analytical reasoning, clear communication, and effective teamwork. Moreover, they will instill resilience, guiding you through both victories and setbacks with grace and determination. All of this takes place in a vibrant, supportive setting that combines educational advancement with the excitement of competition.
Case competitions provide an opportunity for students to be immersed in situations that mirror life in the business world: analysis under pressure, communication, teamwork and, especially, handling success and failure with grace and grit. All of this in a safe, supportive, and fun environment.
— Stéphane Brutus Dean of the Telfer
School of Management

Case competition preparation courses
ADM 3993 Case study analysis and presentation (3 units)
Develop strategic perspective and contribute to the understanding and integration of business activities by introducing new analytical tools and enabling the development of communication skills through a case study approach. Prepares students to participate in case competitions and operate in high performance organizations by simulating executive level analysis and presentations. Case study topics combine the various business issues of small and large enterprises (strategic direction, growth, human resources, financial issues, international business, entrepreneurship).
Course component: Lecture
Prerequisites: All administration (ADM) core courses at 1000 and 2000 levels.
ADM 3994 Preparation and participation to business cases I (3 units)
Preparation and participation for a case competition. Analysis of a variety of business cases. Training in topics that are specific to the case competition. Admission is restricted and selection is made by a committee and/or the professor who will coach the case team.
Course component: Theory and laboratory
Prerequisites: The course ADM 3993 is corequisite to ADM3994. Reserved for students enrolled in third or fourth year having maintained a CGPA of at least 6.0 and completed all 1000 and 2000 level core courses in administration (ADM) of their option.
ADM 4994 Preparation and participation to business cases II (3 units)
Preparation and participation for a case competition. Analysis of a variety of business cases. Training in topics that are specific to the case competition.
Course component: Tutorial
Prerequisite: ADM 3994. Admission is restricted and selection is made by a committee and/or the professor who will coach the case team.

One More Title for FSA ULaval Delegations: The Happening Marketing
Tuesday March 28th, 2023
From March 24 to 26, FSA ULaval and the Université Laval campus welcomed nearly 550 individuals from 10 universities. The reason: the 2023 edition of the Happening Marketing, the largest marketing competition in Eastern Canada, of which the Faculty was the host. The local team did much more than host: it won the competition!
The FSA ULaval delegation made its mark with its consistent performance, achieving 6 podium finishes out of the 8 academic categories:
- Experiential Marketing: 1st place
- Surprise Case: 2nd place
- Strategic Marketing: 2nd place
- Contract: 3rd place
- Digital Marketing: 3rd place
- Quiz: 3rd place
This outstanding academic performance was rounded out by 3rd place finishes in the social and contribution components, which meant that FSA ULaval was able to shine in all of the competition’s components. In addition, Alex Richard, a student in the Bachelor of Business Administration program, was named Volunteer of the Year at the event.
Congratulations to all of the delegation members and the FSA ULaval Competition Committee (CCFSA), coaches, and everyone who supported the participants!

HEC Montréal takes the podium at Happening Marketing
March 27, 2025
The HEC Montréal delegation won six podiums and came in 3rd in the overall rankings of Happening Marketing 2025, which is regarded as the leading interuniversity marketing competition in Eastern Canada. Moreover, the Committee of the Year Award was presented to the HEC Montréal Competitions Committee, and Elizabeth Fortin, a member of the Sports team, was named MVP (Most Valuable Player).
Nearly 600 people from 12 Canadian universities participated in the event that took place from March 21 to 23, 2025 at UQAM’s School of Management (ESG UQAM), under the theme “Notre dernière danse” [Our last dance]. The School team, comprising over 40 Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) students, won plaudits for its creativity and high-quality presentations.
HEC Montréal thus became one of only two academic institutions in 2024-2025 to be ranked in the top 3 of the four competitions of the Regroupement étudiant des facultés d’administration de l’est du Canada [Students’ Association of Management Faculties of Eastern Canada] (RÉFAEC): Sommet GRH (1st place), Jeux du commerce (2nd), Financial Open (3rd), Happening Marketing (3rd).

COMPETITIONS
DESAUTELS MANAGEMENT COMPETITIONS COMMITTEE
The DMCC’s mission is to increase the competitiveness of our faculty at external case competitions, including the Jeux du Commerce, Financial Open and Happening Marketing. In order to do so, we utilize a rigorous selection process which allows us to select the best and brightest of Desautels to represent McGill. Our committee composes of extremely dedicated and enthusiastic individuals who strongly believe in the quality of Desautels students. We are confident that with appropriate training and adequate practice, our delegations will be able to outperform at all competitions. We will be working hard throughout the year to ensure that all delegates have the best possible experiences and are able to represent McGill with pride.
CASE LEAGUE
Case League brings sponsors newfound opportunity to connect with incoming talent of the faculty. Taking place over the course of the entire academic year, corporate partners will have the invaluable opportunity of watching students grow academically, redact original cases for the students to solve, and have heavy influence upon their career choices.
JEUX DU COMMERCE
Connections are one of the most precious assets business people can have. This is why the Commerce Games/Jeux du Commerce aims to help students to develop their business network with companies, but also with the other participants. It also aims to get students to take initiatives and learn how to compete respectfully, and it also encourages excellence. The Commerce Games/Jeux du Commerce is a three-day case competition taking place in the province of Quebec since 1989. Every year, 13 universities counting more than 1040 participants compete in the academic, sportive, and social components.
FINANCIAL OPEN
Financial Open (Omnium Financier) is an inter-university academic case competition that brings together the best students in finance and accounting from over 10 schools across Eastern Canada. Students test their business and presentation skills in six finance and accounting cases, in a quiz, as well as in a stock simulation. The Financial Open also presents an excellent opportunity to network with sponsoring companies.
DESAUTELS WOMEN IN BUSINESS
Desautels Women in Business aims to provide a platform for students to explore the opportunities and careers available to them. We aim to create a strong network of women interested in a career in management; this network consists of students, McGill alumni, Professors, and current industry professionals.
HAPPENING MARKETING
Happening Marketing is a marketing completion that brings together 10 universities and more than 500 participants all over Quebec. The event is comprised of two main categories, academics and sports, as well as a variety of other social events. In preparation for the competition, students will participate in various practices and be coached by esteemed members of the business community. Throughout the course of the academic year, delegates will have the opportunity to bond with their fellow classmates and develop their presentation, analytical skills. On the sports team, athletes will work over the year to learn a new sport and get in physical shape. Not only does this competition allow participants to show their skills in a competitive context, it is also an amazing opportunity for them to meet and network with various judges and company executives, as well as other students from all over the province.
DESAUTELS MANAGEMENT LEADERSHIP SEMINAR
Over the course of one weekend, students will be enriched a variety of multidisciplinary events in a breadth of fields, including accounting, finance, marketing, entrepreneurship, consulting, and much more. Day 1 will consist of a speaker series with speakers from a variety of industries sharing their experiences and insights. Day 2 is a case competition, where teams will crack a case in 2 hours with the help of mentor to teach the fundamentals of case cracking. DMLS offers the chance to gain valuable skills and knowledge which will be the stepping stones to your future at Desautels.
DESAUTELS preparatory case competition
The Desautels Preparatory Case Competition is a student run case competition whose mission is to actively promote higher education and leadership amongst CEGEP and high school students, with a focus on the personal development opportunities provided by a business education. The goal of DPCC is to provide CEGEP students with a better understanding of not only what a business degree entails but also introduce them to our faculty’s excellence and the opportunities it provides.
Bolt
BOLT is a competitive business tech bootcamp aimed to integrate top business and tech talent and enable emerging leaders to pursue innovation in a whole new light through the development of products and solutions of real impact. BOLT has hosted over 100+ undergraduate students, delivering specialized workshops in entrepreneurship, design thinking, product development, and UX/UI design.
