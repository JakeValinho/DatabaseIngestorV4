About Us
CFAC Portfolio Management is a student-ran investment fund, soley managed by undergraduate
students attending the University of British Columbia in Vancouver, BC. Founded in 2016, CFAC's
objective is to educate members on the fundamentals of Capital Markets while raising awareness
and funds for ALS Research.  
 
CFAC provides students with experience in managing an investment portfolio, and education in
capital markets. CFAC provides students with experimental education and investment
management through allocation of its investment portfolio. Since 2022, CFAC has hosted annual
stock pitch competitions, to educate students across Canada.
CFAC Portfolio Management is proud to announce its inaugural 2025 Investment Banking Case
Competition. 
From March 31st to April 4th, competitors will be responsible for simulating the responsibilities
and deliverables of an investment banking professional engaged on an M&A transaction. Teams
will be able to compete for a prize pool of $1,000.00, and if selected for the finals, present live to
investment professionals at the University of British Columbia. Open to all undergraduate
students registered in domestic post-secondary programs, competitors can compete in teams of
a maximum of four members. Registration is $100.00/group-entry. All teams will receive acsess to
the official case package on March 31th.
  
Should you have any questions or concerns, please feel free to reach out or contact us at
cfacubc@gmail.com.  
Best of luck! - CFAC Portfolio Management‘s Exec Team

Pitch Guidelines
Investment banking competitions challenge participants to apply financial analysis, valuation
techniques, and strategic thinking in order to solve real-world business challenges. Simulating the
role of an investment banking team, competitors are required to analyze a company's financials,
assess market trends, propose strategic recommendations, and summarize their findings through
the creation of a pitchbook. 
The basic structure of a pitch is as follows: a company and industry analysis, valuation and assessment
of the company’s financial position, and finally alternatives and recommendations. We recommend
new or inexperienced competitors review CFAC’s resources on stock pitches and previous reports
provided by CFAC’s investment team. 
Guidelines to CFAC’s 2025 competition regarding requirements for slide-deck length, specific
presentation sections, and other related information, are scheduled to be released in the official case-
package after registration closes, by March 31st, 2025. This year’s case will focus on the M&A of a
Canadian company. After the case release, competitors will have five days to create a pitchbook with
valuation models and strategic recommendations. The completed submission must be submitted
online through the form provided by April 4, 11:59 PM PST.
